| Attribute | Target |

| Walk-in percentage | >50% of customers |

**Specific restaurant archetypes to target first:**

2. **The No-Reservation Parilla** -- Traditional policy of no reservations. Generates sidewalk crowds. Examples: Don Julio model (keeps walk-in tables), neighborhood parillas.
3. **The Trendy New Opening** -- First 6-12 months. Buzz-driven. Long waits but no infrastructure to manage them. Constantly featured in Timeout/Infobae food sections.
4. **The Wine Bar with Small Plates** -- 20-30 seats. Fills up fast. Loses groups who don't want to wait outside.

### 1.4 Pricing for Argentina

**The Inflation Problem:**
- Argentina YoY inflation: ~33% (Feb 2026)
- Restaurant & hotel sector inflation: ~41.6% YoY
- ARS/USD: ~1,400 pesos per dollar (March 2026), band floating between 1,000-1,400
- SaaS in Argentina is almost universally priced in USD or USD-pegged
- Restaurant owners think in pesos but accept that software is priced in dollars

**Pricing Strategy: USD pricing, billed in ARS at daily exchange rate**

| Plan | USD Price | ARS Equivalent (~1,400/USD) | What's Included |
|------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| **Starter (Free)** | $0/mo | $0 | Up to 50 waitlist joins/month, basic WhatsApp notifications, 1 location |
| **Pro** | $29/mo | ~ARS 40,600 | Unlimited waitlist joins, priority notifications, analytics dashboard, estimated wait times, 1 location |
| **Growth** | $59/mo | ~ARS 82,600 | Everything in Pro + multi-location, custom branding, API access, priority support |

**Why these prices:**
- $29/mo = cost of ~2 covers at a mid-range BA restaurant. If Meantime saves even 2 walk-aways per month, it pays for itself.
- Average restaurant in BA doing 80-150 covers on a busy night at ARS 15,000-30,000 average ticket = ARS 1.2M-4.5M revenue on one peak night. $29/mo is noise.
- Comparable SaaS in Argentina: POS systems charge $30-80/mo. Delivery platforms charge 15-30% commission. $29 flat fee is cheap by comparison.

**Billing mechanics:**
- Accept payment via Mercado Pago (most common in Argentina), wire transfer, or international card
- Bill in USD, charge ARS equivalent at Mercado Pago's exchange rate
- Annual prepay discount: 2 months free (pay 10 months, get 12) = $290/year instead of $348

**What Hormozi would charge:**
Alex Hormozi's framework says price on the value, not the cost. If Meantime saves a restaurant 5 lost tables per week at ARS 60,000 average ticket = ARS 300,000/week = ARS 1.2M/month in recovered revenue. Charging $29/mo for $857/mo in value = 29x ROI. Hormozi would say you could charge $99-149/mo and still deliver a 10x+ return. BUT -- for Phase 1 market entry in Argentina, $29 removes price objections and gets you to 10 restaurants fast. Raise prices later with data proving ROI.

### 4.2 Recommended Entry Order

**Month 12-15: Colombia (Bogota + Medellin)**
- Why first: Spanish-speaking (no translation needed), high WhatsApp penetration (94%), growing restaurant scene, low competition in digital waitlists, Colombian peso more stable than ARS, Bogota and Medellin have dense restaurant neighborhoods (Zona T, Zona G, El Poblado, Laureles)
- Localization: Minimal. Adjust currency (COP), payment methods (PSE, Nequi, Daviplata), local Spanish variations
- Pricing: $19-39/mo USD (Colombian restaurants have lower average tickets than Argentine ones)

**Month 15-18: Mexico (CDMX + Guadalajara)**
- Why second: 93% WhatsApp penetration. CDMX has one of the largest restaurant markets in the world (Condesa, Roma, Polanco have extreme wait cultures). Mexico represents the biggest revenue opportunity.
- Localization: Mexican Spanish adjustments, payment integration (OXXO, SPEI, Clip), different regulatory environment for data (LFPDPPP)
- Pricing: $25-49/mo USD
- Competition: Woki and Reservandonos exist but focus on reservations, not waitlists

**Month 18-24: Brazil (Sao Paulo + Rio)**
- Why third (not first despite biggest market): Portuguese language requires full translation, different payment ecosystem (PIX, Boleto), Get In is an established competitor in the waitlist space, Brazil requires LGPD compliance (data protection), CNPJ registration may be needed
- Localization: Full Portuguese translation, PIX payment integration, LGPD compliance, Brazilian WhatsApp norms (they use WhatsApp more than anyone)
- Pricing: R$99-199/mo (BRL)
- Strategy: Differentiate from Get In by being simpler, WhatsApp-native (Get In uses its own app)

**Month 20-24: Chile (Santiago)**
- Why later: Smaller market but stable economy. Good for profitability.
- Localization: Minimal (Chilean Spanish adjustments, payment via Webpay/Transbank)
- Pricing: $25-45/mo USD (or CLP equivalent)

### 4.4 Competitive Landscape by Country

**Argentina:** Near-zero direct competition for WhatsApp-native waitlists. RAY handles reservations. OpenTable has minimal presence outside fine dining. This is blue ocean.

**Colombia:** No established digital waitlist player. Some restaurants use Google Forms or Instagram DMs to manage waits. Big opportunity.

**Mexico:** Woki and Reservandonos focus on reservations, not waitlists. OpenTable has presence in high-end CDMX restaurants. Waitlist management is mostly manual (pen and paper or shouting names).

**Brazil:** Get In is the primary competitor -- focused on real-time waitlists. However, Get In requires app download from diners. Meantime's advantage: WhatsApp-native, zero friction, no app download. Significant differentiation.

**Chile:** Minimal competition. Some OpenTable in Santiago fine dining. Waitlists are managed manually.

## 2.5 Pricing Strategy Deep Dive

### Freemium vs Paid-Only

**Recommendation: Freemium with tight limits.**

| Aspect | Freemium | Paid-Only |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Adoption speed | Fast | Slow |
| Trust building | Restaurant tries before paying | Asks for trust upfront |
| Conversion pressure | Product must prove value | Sales must prove value |
| Argentine market fit | HIGH (Argentines are skeptical of new tools and want to try first) | LOW (hard sell in a market with trust issues) |

**Free tier limits (designed to force upgrade on successful restaurants):**
- 50 waitlist joins/month (a busy restaurant on one Friday night could hit this)
- Basic notifications only (no estimated wait times, no analytics)
- Meantime branding on customer-facing messages
- This means: free tier works for slow restaurants (who you don't want to pay anyway). Busy restaurants hit the limit in 1-2 weekends and upgrade.

### USD vs ARS Pricing

**Price in USD. Always.**
- Every SaaS in Argentina that has survived inflation prices in USD
- Restaurant owners understand this -- their food costs are partially dollarized (imported ingredients)
- Accept payment in ARS at daily exchange rate via Mercado Pago
- Do NOT price in ARS. At 33%+ annual inflation, you would need to raise prices every month, creating constant friction and churn.

### Monthly vs Annual

- **Monthly:** Default option. Lower commitment. Reduces friction in a market where restaurant cash flow is unpredictable.
- **Annual:** Offer 2 months free ($290/year vs $348). Position as "lock in your price" -- appealing in an inflationary environment.
- **Expected split:** 70% monthly / 30% annual by Month 12.
