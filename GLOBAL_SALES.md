## 1.1 SaaS Marketplaces & Directories

### Capterra
- **How to list**: Free basic listing at capterra.com/vendors. Upload product info, screenshots, pricing.
- **Paid placement**: PPC model. Minimum $2/click, $500/month minimum budget (second-price auction). Per-country and per-category bidding.
- **Recommended starting budget**: $1,000-2,000/month to gather performance data before scaling.
- **Expected traffic**: Capterra is owned by Gartner and is one of the top software discovery sites. The "Waitlist Software" category page (capterra.com/waitlist-software) lists dozens of competitors. Getting into the top 5 with paid placement is realistic.
- **Conversion rates**: Expect 2-5% click-to-trial from Capterra traffic (higher intent than organic search).
- **Strategy**: Collect 10-15 genuine reviews from early users BEFORE activating paid placement. Reviews are the #1 trust signal.

### G2
- **How to list**: Free profile at sell.g2.com. Claim your product, add details.
- **Paid tiers**: Annual subscriptions start at $2,999/year first year, $6,000/year on renewal for companies under 50 employees. Monthly billing available at $299/month.
- **PPC add-on**: $2/click minimum, $500/month minimum (same model as Capterra -- both owned by Gartner Digital Markets).
- **Expected traffic**: G2 carries millions of verified reviews. Important for mid-market and enterprise buyers who compare solutions.
- **Strategy**: Prioritize Capterra first (better for SMB restaurant buyers), add G2 when targeting chains/enterprise.

### GetApp / Software Advice
- **How to list**: Both are Gartner Digital Markets properties (same parent as Capterra). Listing on Capterra often syndicates to these.
- **Cost**: Similar PPC model as Capterra.
- **Value**: Software Advice offers a phone-based advisory service that recommends products to buyers -- high-intent leads.

### TrustRadius
- **How to list**: Free profile. More enterprise-focused than Capterra.
- **Cost**: Paid tiers for intent data and lead generation available.
- **Expected value for Meantime**: Lower priority. More relevant when targeting chains with 50+ locations.

### Product Hunt
- **Timing strategy**: Launch at 12:01 AM PST to maximize the full 24-hour window. Avoid weekends. Check for major competing launches.
- **Pre-launch prep**: Build a list of 500+ people ready to upvote/comment on day one. First 3 hours are critical for the algorithm.
- **Restaurant SaaS examples**: Tools like Eat App, Owner.com, and Popmenu have launched on PH.
- **Realistic expectations**: PH's filtering has gotten very selective in 2025. Don't expect overnight virality. Optimize for email capture, not vanity upvotes.
- **Best use**: Brand awareness + backlinks for SEO. Expect 500-2,000 website visits on launch day. 5-15% email capture rate.
- **Follow-up**: Run an email drip sequence to convert PH visitors within 7 days.

### AppSumo
- **Model**: AppSumo keeps 70% of revenue; you get 30%.
- **Revenue examples**: Feedhive made $400K; Frase made $800K in 28 days and added 8,000 customers.
- **The math problem**: A $59 lifetime deal nets you ~$17.70 after AppSumo's cut. These users consume server/SMS resources for years.
- **Pros**: Massive user acquisition burst, social proof, reviews, feedback.
- **Cons**: Attracts deal-hunters, not ideal customers. Lifetime deal users have 70%+ churn on engagement. Can create unsustainable support burden.
- **Recommendation for Meantime**: Consider ONLY if you need a rapid influx of users for social proof. Offer a capped plan (e.g., 1 location, 50 parties/day). DO NOT offer unlimited usage lifetime deals.

### Shopify App Store Equivalent for Restaurants
- There is no single dominant "app store for restaurants." The POS-specific marketplaces (see Section 1.2) are the closest equivalent. Other directories:
  - **Restaurant365 Marketplace** (restaurant365.com)
  - **Foodservice Tech Stack** directories like RestaurantTechnologyGuys.com
  - **Hospitality Technology** magazine listings (htmagazine.com)

## 1.2 App Store / Integration Marketplaces

### Toast Marketplace
- **URL**: pos.toasttab.com/partners
- **Requirements**: Partner agreement with Toast compliance/privacy/security/legal teams. Must have mutual customers requesting the integration. Subscription to Restaurant Management Essentials or Pro required for API access.
- **Revenue share**: Referral partners get one-time cash bounties ($500 local partner, $1,000 first location). Integration partners get marketplace visibility but no direct revenue share -- you bill restaurants directly.
- **Application process**: Review API Documentation License Agreement, complete integration partner application form.
- **Expected volume**: Toast has 120,000+ restaurant locations. Top integration partners see thousands of installs.
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months from application to live listing (compliance review, development, QA).
- **Priority**: HIGH for US market. Toast is the dominant US restaurant POS.

### Square App Marketplace
- **URL**: squareup.com/us/en/partnerships/app-partner
- **Requirements**: Build with Square APIs, submit app for review.
- **Revenue share**: You set your own pricing. Square does not take a cut of your subscription revenue.
- **Expected volume**: Square has millions of sellers, including many restaurants (especially SMB/independent).
- **Priority**: HIGH. Easiest marketplace to get into. Great for independent restaurants globally.

### Clover App Market
- **Revenue share**: 70% royalties to the developer on App Market subscriptions (Clover takes 30%).
- **Requirements**: Build using Clover APIs, submit for approval.
- **Expected volume**: Clover has ~700,000 merchants (many restaurants in US/Canada).
- **Priority**: MEDIUM. Clover restaurants tend to be smaller independent operators.

### Lightspeed Restaurant
- **URL**: lightspeedhq.com/ecom/app-market-partners
- **Model**: Referral partner program with one-time payouts or recurring commissions depending on track/region.
- **Requirements**: API integration, certification, marketplace listing.
- **Priority**: MEDIUM. Strong in Canada, Australia, and parts of Europe.

### Google Reserve (Reserve with Google)
- **URL**: developers.google.com/actions-center/verticals/reservations/e2e/overview
- **Requirements**:
  - Direct contractual relationship with merchants
  - Real-time access to merchant availability (respond to Google in <1 second)
  - Minimum 30 days of availability inventory
  - GDPR compliance
  - Online cancellation support
- **How to become a partner**: Fill out the Partner interest form via Actions Center. Work with assigned Google contact.
- **Revenue share**: No direct revenue share for the integration. Google drives free traffic to your merchants.
- **Expected value**: VERY HIGH. Restaurants appear as "Reserve" buttons directly in Google Search and Maps. Massive visibility.
- **Timeline**: 6-12 months for full integration. Technically complex.
- **Priority**: HIGH. This is a competitive moat once you have it.

### Shopify POS
- **Relevance**: Shopify POS is used by some restaurants but is primarily a retail POS. Lower priority for Meantime.
- **Priority**: LOW.

### Recommended Model for Meantime: Freemium + Self-Serve Upgrade

**Why freemium beats free trial for restaurant software:**
- Restaurant owners are risk-averse and busy. They won't commit to a 14-day trial they might forget.
- A forever-free tier (1 location, basic features, Meantime branding) creates ongoing usage and word-of-mouth.
- Freemium drives 12% median visitor-to-signup conversion (vs 8.5% for opt-in trials, 2.5% for opt-out trials).

**Conversion benchmarks to plan against:**
- Visitor to signup: 8-12%
- Signup to activation (first waitlist created): 40-60% (with good onboarding)
- Free to paid conversion: 5-9% (freemium), 18-25% (opt-in trial), 48% (opt-out/card-required trial)
- Users who complete onboarding are 5x more likely to convert

**Self-service onboarding best practices:**
1. Sign up with Google/email in under 30 seconds
2. Skip the demo -- let them create their first waitlist immediately
3. Show the QR code/link they can use with guests within 2 minutes
4. Send first guest notification within the trial to demonstrate value
5. Activation milestone: first real guest added to waitlist
6. Trigger upgrade prompt after 50 parties managed (value proven)

### Stripe Billing for Global Payments
- Stripe supports 135+ currencies and 46 countries
- For LATAM: Stripe supports Brazil (Pix, boleto), Mexico (OXXO), Argentina (limited -- consider MercadoPago as alternative)
- MercadoPago for Argentina, Brazil, Mexico, Chile, Colombia, Peru, Uruguay
- Implement Stripe + MercadoPago dual billing to maximize LATAM coverage
- Offer monthly and annual billing (annual at 15-20% discount to improve LTV)

## 2.1 Latin America

### Argentina
- **Restaurants**: ~100,000+ food establishments
- **WhatsApp penetration**: 90%+ (dominant messaging app)
- **Restaurant tech spend**: Very low ($20-50/month typical for any SaaS). Economic instability limits spending.
- **Key competitors**: Very few dedicated waitlist apps. Most restaurants use WhatsApp manually or pen-and-paper.
- **Regulatory**: Frequent currency controls, import/export restrictions on software services. Dollar-denominated pricing is problematic (use ARS or USD at parallel rate).
- **Payment methods**: MercadoPago (dominant), bank transfer, cash. Stripe has limited support. Credit card installments ("cuotas") essential.
- **Language**: Spanish (Rioplatense dialect)
- **Opportunity**: HIGH. WhatsApp-native waitlist is a perfect fit. Almost no competition.

### Brazil
- **Restaurants**: 1 million+ food establishments (largest market in LATAM, 35% of regional revenue)
- **WhatsApp penetration**: 85-90%+ (used by virtually everyone)
- **Restaurant tech spend**: $30-100/month. Growing adoption of digital tools.
- **Key competitors**: TagMe (Brazilian waitlist/reservation system), GetIn (queue management), international players like Eat App
- **Regulatory**: LGPD (data protection law similar to GDPR). Must store data locally or comply with cross-border transfer rules.
- **Payment methods**: Pix (instant payment, used by 150M+ Brazilians), boleto, credit cards, MercadoPago
- **Language**: Brazilian Portuguese
- **Opportunity**: VERY HIGH. Massive market, WhatsApp-native, Pix for billing.

### Mexico
- **Restaurants**: 600,000+ food establishments
- **WhatsApp penetration**: 90%+
- **Restaurant tech spend**: $30-80/month
- **Key competitors**: Limited dedicated waitlist solutions. Some restaurants use CoverManager (Spanish company).
- **Regulatory**: Standard business operation. Facturacion electronica (electronic invoicing) required for B2B.
- **Payment methods**: OXXO (cash payment network), SPEI (bank transfers), credit cards, Stripe Mexico, MercadoPago
- **Language**: Mexican Spanish
- **Opportunity**: HIGH. Large market, growing restaurant tech adoption.

### Colombia
- **Restaurants**: 300,000+
- **WhatsApp penetration**: 90%+
- **Restaurant tech spend**: $20-50/month
- **Key competitors**: Very limited. Rappi dominates delivery but no dedicated waitlist.
- **Payment methods**: PSE (bank transfer), Nequi, Daviplata, credit cards
- **Language**: Colombian Spanish
- **Opportunity**: MEDIUM-HIGH.

### Chile

## 5.1 Pricing Philosophy

**Key principle**: Companies implementing PPP-adjusted regional pricing see 4.7x higher conversion in emerging markets and 25% higher revenue per customer vs. uniform global pricing.

**Recommendation**: 3-tier pricing (Free / Pro / Enterprise) with regional PPP adjustment.

**Discount multipliers by region** (relative to US pricing):

| Region | PPP Multiplier | Rationale |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| US / Canada | 1.0x (base) | Highest willingness to pay |
| UK / Western Europe | 0.85-0.95x | Similar purchasing power, slight EUR/GBP adjustment |
| Australia / NZ | 0.85-0.90x | Similar to UK |
| Southern Europe (Spain, Italy, Portugal, Greece) | 0.65-0.75x | Lower restaurant margins |
| Middle East (UAE, Saudi) | 0.80-0.90x | Premium market, high spending |
| Turkey | 0.40-0.50x | Currency volatility, lower purchasing power |
| LATAM (Brazil, Mexico, Colombia) | 0.35-0.50x | Significant purchasing power gap |
| LATAM (Argentina) | 0.25-0.35x | Severe economic challenges, currency instability |
| Southeast Asia | 0.25-0.40x | Low ARPU but high volume potential |
| India | 0.20-0.30x | Lowest price needed, highest volume |
| Africa | 0.25-0.40x | Emerging market |
